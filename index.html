Across the nation, people in both red states and blue states are realizing that modernizing our cities with 100% clean and renewable energy will help create jobs and boost local economies. A recent economic study estimated that a transition to clean energy will add 1 million jobs in the U.S. by 2030 and increase household disposable income by $350-$400 in 2030 and by as much as $650 in 2050.

The renewable energy sector is booming. Wind and solar prices have rapidly declined in the past five years, and the solar industry is one of the fastest growing sectors of the US economy.

Poll after poll shows that a majority of Americans support renewable energy. In a recent national survey of U.S. adults paid for by the Sierra Club, a whopping 83 percent of respondents said they support a goal of 100% clean energy.

What kinds of renewable energy are there?

Solar

Solar energy harnesses the power of the sun to create energy, which then can be stored in local batteries.

Wind

Wind energy utilizes the motion of wind to create electricity, which then gets transferred across energy grids.

Biomass

Biomass is a group of technologies that use living or recently-living organisms and waste to create energy.

Hydropower

Hydropower uses the kinetic energy in moving water, such as in rivers, to create power.

Geothermal

Geothermal technologies use the internal heat of the Earth to create energy.
